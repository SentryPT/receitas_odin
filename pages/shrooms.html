Crispy mushroom parmigiana salad

This salad version of the pub classic, parmigiana, is quick, healthy and tasty. Easily make it vegan by subbing the bocconcini for vegan cheese and milk for nut mylk.

    Ingredients
  • 2 eggs, lightly whisked
  • 80ml (1/3 cup) milk
  • 50g (1/3 cup) plain flour
  • 50g (1 cup) panko breadcrumbs
  • 8 large flat mushrooms, stalks removed
  • Vegetable oil, to shallow fry
  • 60ml (1/4 cup) olive oil
  • 1 tbs balsamic vinegar
  • 1 garlic clove, crushed
  • 120g mixed salad leaves
  • 220g tub bocconcini, drained, coarsely torn
  • 4 small roma tomatoes, cut into wedges
  • 1 small red onion, cut into thin wedges
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 40g (1/2 cup) shredded parmesan
  • 2 tbs pine nuts, toasted
    Steps
  1. Whisk together the egg. No need to flip back and forth!
  2. Place flour on a plate and season. Place the breadcrumbs on a separate plate. Working with 1 mushroom at a time, coat in flour and shake off excess. Dip in the egg mixture then the breadcrumbs, pressing firmly to coat. Transfer to a plate. Repeat to double crumb the mushrooms. Place in the fridge until chilled.
  3. Add the vegetable oil to a medium non-stick frying pan to come 1cm up side of pan. Heat over medium-high heat and cook mushrooms, in batches, for 3-4 minutes each side or until golden. Transfer to a wire rack to drain.
  4. Whisk together the olive oil, vinegar and garlic in a small bowl. Season. Divide the salad leaves, mushrooms, bocconcini, tomato, onion and basil among serving plates. Sprinkle with the parmesan and pine nuts. Drizzle over the dressing to serve.

Nutrition Facts
562 calories per serve Allergens: Recipe may contain yeast, sulphites, gluten, wheat and tree nut.
